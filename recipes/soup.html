SOUP

Description

To make a hearty and comforting chicken soup, gather the following ingredients: bone-in, skin-on chicken pieces (such as thighs or drumsticks), carrots, celery, onions, garlic, fresh thyme, bay leaves, salt, pepper, and chicken broth. Start by seasoning the chicken pieces with salt and pepper. In a large pot, heat some olive oil over medium heat and brown the chicken on all sides to enhance flavor. Remove the chicken and sauté chopped onions, garlic, carrots, and celery until they become tender. Return the chicken to the pot, add fresh thyme and bay leaves, and pour in enough chicken broth to cover the ingredients. Bring the soup to a simmer, reduce the heat, and let it cook until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.

Once the chicken and vegetables are cooked to perfection, remove the chicken from the pot and shred the meat using two forks. Discard the bones and skin. Return the shredded chicken to the soup, and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to taste. If desired, you can add cooked noodles or rice for a heartier soup. Let the soup simmer for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to meld together. Before serving, remove the bay leaves and thyme stems. Ladle the warm and comforting chicken soup into bowls, garnish with fresh parsley, and serve with a slice of crusty bread for a wholesome and delicious meal that's perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • Bone-in, skin-on chicken pieces (such as thighs or drumsticks)
  • Carrots
  • Celery
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Fresh thyme
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Chicken broth

Steps

  1. Season chicken pieces with salt and pepper.
  2. Brown the chicken in a pot with olive oil.
  3. Remove chicken and sauté onions, garlic, carrots, and celery.
  4. Return chicken to the pot, add thyme, bay leaves, and chicken broth.
  5. Simmer until chicken is cooked and vegetables are tender.
  6. Remove chicken, shred the meat, and return it to the pot.
  7. Adjust seasoning and add cooked noodles or rice if desired.
  8. Simmer for a few more minutes to meld flavors.
  9. Remove bay leaves and thyme stems before serving.
  10. Ladle soup into bowls, garnish with parsley, and serve.
